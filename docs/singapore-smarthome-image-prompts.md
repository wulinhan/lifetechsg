# Singapore Smart-Home Image Prompts

Generation-ready prompts for authentic Singaporean smart-home imagery across the
Life Tech SG landing page. Each entry lists **where it goes**, the **aspect ratio /
target size** for that slot, and a copy-paste **prompt**.

## How to use

1. Paste a prompt into your image generator (Midjourney, DALL·E, Firefly, etc.).
2. Append the **shared style suffix** below to every prompt so the whole set looks
   like one brand shoot.
3. Export at (or above) the target size, then drop the file into the path noted
   under each image and tell me the filename — I'll wire it in.

### Shared style suffix (append to every prompt)

> photorealistic interior photography, natural warm tropical daylight or cosy
> evening light, shallow depth of field, editorial real-estate style, calm and
> uncluttered, warm neutral palette with subtle matte-black and soft-gold accents,
> 35mm, high detail, no on-screen text, no visible brand logos, no watermark

### Negative / avoid

> cluttered, cartoonish, obvious CGI, Western/snowy exteriors, fake glossy render,
> distorted architecture, text overlays, garish colours, cheap stock-photo feel

### Singapore authenticity cues to keep in shots

- HDB flats: full-height windows with neighbouring blocks or rain-tree greenery
  outside, light oak laminate flooring, service yard, bomb-shelter door, tropical
  overcast or golden-hour light.
- Condos: floor-to-ceiling glass, distant city or pool view, sintered-stone counters.
- Renovation style: warm Scandinavian-minimalist, one dark feature wall, track
  lighting, marble-look surfaces.
- People: everyday Singaporean homeowners of varied ethnicities (Chinese, Malay,
  Indian, Eurasian), late-20s to 50s, relaxed and natural, sometimes a family.
- Product cues: sleek matte rocker light switches (black or white), a wall LCD
  smart panel by the entrance, motorised curtains, a slim ceiling or corner fan, a
  small IR hub near the TV/aircon, one phone running a single home app.

### Consistency tips
- Generate the whole set in one session with the same style suffix so lighting and
  palette match.
- Keep switch plates **matte** (not glossy) and mostly black or white to match the
  real products.
- One phone, one app — never show several competing apps (that's the "open, not
  locked-in" story).
- Golden-hour or soft-evening light reads warmest and most "effortless living".
