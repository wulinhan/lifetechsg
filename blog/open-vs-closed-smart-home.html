Open vs closed smart homes: Tuya, Aqara, and Apple Home.

Short answer: an open platform lets you mix brands in one app. A closed one locks you in. We build open.

Closed ecosystems.

Aqara is the popular example. It works well, as long as you buy Aqara for everything and run it through the Aqara app. Step outside the brand and things stop fitting together. Neat, but narrow.

Open platforms.

Our system runs on Tuya, an open platform. A fan from one brand, switches from another, a sensor from a third, all in one app. You're not tied to a single maker, and you can pick the best device for each job. That's how we run real setups: quality smart switches, smart fans, and other Tuya-compatible gear, one app, with the same price and warranty across the switch brands we carry.

What about Apple HomeKit?

For most people, no. It's technically possible through a specific gateway, but it's fiddly and we don't recommend it for general use. The system is built for Google and Alexa. If your household lives in Apple Home, tell us early and we'll talk it through honestly.

Why it matters when you buy.

A closed system feels simple on day one and expensive later, when you want to add something the brand doesn't make. Open keeps your choices open. Less exciting, more practical.
